EMPLOYEE ONBOARDING PROCESS

Welcome to the company! Here's what to expect during your first week and beyond:

PRE-FIRST DAY PREPARATION

Before You Start:
- Complete pre-employment paperwork (sent via email)
- Review employee handbook (available online)
- Set up direct deposit information
- Prepare required documents (see list below)
- Complete background check (if applicable)
- Review first week schedule (sent 3 days before start date)
- Download company mobile app
- Set up company email account (instructions sent separately)

Required Documents (Bring on Day 1):
- Government-issued ID (driver's license or passport)
- Social Security card or work authorization document
- Bank account information (for direct deposit): Account number and routing number
- Emergency contact information: Name, relationship, phone number
- Proof of address (utility bill or lease)
- Voided check or bank statement (for direct deposit)
- Tax forms: W-4 (federal), state tax form (if applicable)
- I-9 form: Employment eligibility verification
- Direct deposit form: Bank account details

DAY 1 - ORIENTATION

Welcome Meeting with HR (9:00 AM - 10:00 AM):
- Company overview and history
- Mission, vision, and values presentation
- Organizational structure introduction
- Benefits overview and enrollment information
- Review of employee handbook
- Q&A session with HR team
- Tour of office facilities
- Introduction to key personnel

IT Setup and Equipment Distribution (10:00 AM - 12:00 PM):
- Laptop/computer assignment and setup
- Email account activation
- Password setup and security training
- Software installation and access provisioning
- Phone setup (if applicable)
- Access card/key fob distribution
- WiFi credentials and network access
- VPN setup (if remote work)
- Two-factor authentication setup
- Introduction to IT support portal

Office Tour and Introductions (1:00 PM - 2:00 PM):
- Office layout and facilities tour
- Break room and kitchen locations
- Restroom locations
- Conference room locations and booking system
- Parking information
- Security procedures
- Emergency exits and procedures
- Introduction to team members
- Introduction to department heads
- Meet your manager one-on-one

Paperwork Completion (2:00 PM - 4:00 PM):
- Complete all required forms
- Benefits enrollment (if eligible)
- Direct deposit setup
- Emergency contact information
- Tax withholding forms
- Acknowledgment of company policies
- Confidentiality agreement
- Code of conduct acknowledgment

DAY 2-3 - TRAINING AND INTEGRATION

Department-Specific Training:
- Role and responsibilities overview
- Department structure and team members
- Key processes and procedures
- Tools and systems used in department
- Workflow and expectations
- Performance metrics and goals
- Department-specific policies

System Access and Credentials:
- Access to all required systems
- Login credentials for all platforms
- Training on primary software tools
- Access to shared drives and folders
- Project management tool training
- Communication platform training (Slack, Teams)
- Calendar and scheduling system
- Time tracking system (if applicable)

Introduction to Team Members:
- Team structure and roles
- One-on-one meetings with team members
- Introduction to cross-functional partners
- Mentor assignment (if applicable)
- Buddy program introduction

Review of Job Responsibilities:
- Detailed job description review
- Key performance indicators (KPIs)
- Success metrics
- 30-60-90 day goals
- Expectations and deliverables
- Reporting structure
- Communication preferences

WEEK 1 - SETTLING IN

Complete All Required Paperwork:
- All forms submitted and processed
- Benefits enrollment completed (if eligible)
- Direct deposit confirmed
- Emergency contacts on file
- Tax forms submitted

Set Up Direct Deposit:
- Bank account verified
- First paycheck information provided
- Pay schedule explained (bi-weekly, every other Friday)
- Pay stub access instructions

Enroll in Benefits (If Eligible):
- Health insurance selection
- Dental and vision enrollment
- 401(k) enrollment (after 90 days)
- Life insurance beneficiary designation
- Review of all benefit options
- Benefits portal access

Complete Mandatory Compliance Training:
- Security awareness training
- Data privacy training (GDPR, CCPA)
- Harassment prevention training
- Code of conduct training
- Safety training
- IT security policies
- All training must be completed within first week

EMPLOYEE HANDBOOK

Code of Conduct:
- Treat all colleagues with respect and professionalism
- Maintain confidentiality of company information
- Follow all company policies and procedures
- Report any violations or concerns to HR or manager
- No discrimination or harassment of any kind
- Professional communication at all times
- Ethical behavior expected
- Conflict of interest disclosure required

Dress Code:
- Business casual attire standard
- No offensive graphics or messages on clothing
- Appropriate footwear required (no flip-flops)
- Remote work: Professional appearance for video calls
- Client-facing roles: Business professional may be required
- Casual Fridays: Jeans allowed (check with manager)
- Safety requirements: Follow department-specific guidelines

Work Hours:
- Standard hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM (flexible)
- Flexible hours available with manager approval
- Core hours: 10:00 AM - 3:00 PM (must be available)
- Overtime requires pre-approval
- Time tracking: Required for hourly employees
- Break times: 15-minute breaks, 1-hour lunch
- Overtime pay: 1.5x for hours over 40 per week

Telecommuting Policy:
- Remote work available with manager approval
- Must have reliable internet connection (minimum 25 Mbps)
- Dedicated workspace required
- Regular check-ins required (daily stand-ups)
- Office attendance required for team meetings (as needed)
- Equipment provided by company
- VPN required for remote access
- Security requirements must be met

PROFESSIONAL DEVELOPMENT

Training Opportunities:
- Annual training budget: $2,000 per employee
- Internal training programs available
- External courses and conferences (with manager approval)
- Online learning platform access (LinkedIn Learning, Coursera, Udemy)
- Department-specific training programs
- Cross-functional training opportunities
- Lunch and learn sessions: Monthly
- Technical workshops: Quarterly

Career Development:
- Annual performance reviews (December)
- Mid-year check-ins (June)
- Individual development plans (IDPs)
- Mentorship programs: Formal and informal
- Internal job postings prioritized for current employees
- Career pathing resources available
- Leadership development programs
- Cross-functional project opportunities

Tuition Reimbursement:
- Up to $5,000 per year for relevant courses
- Must maintain B average or better
- Requires manager approval before enrollment

- Covers: tuition, books, fees
- Graduate programs: Up to $10,000 per year (with approval)
