EMPLOYEE RELATIONS AND PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

EMPLOYEE RELATIONS OVERVIEW

Employee Relations Philosophy:
- Open communication and transparency
- Fair and consistent treatment of all employees
- Respectful workplace environment
- Conflict resolution through dialogue
- Support for employee concerns and feedback
- Proactive management of workplace issues
- Employee advocacy and support

Employee Relations Team:
- HR Business Partners assigned to departments
- Employee Relations Specialists available
- Confidential consultation services
- Mediation services for conflicts
- Investigation of complaints and concerns
- Support during disciplinary processes

COMMUNICATION POLICIES

Open Door Policy:
- Employees can speak with any level of management
- No retaliation for raising concerns
- Confidentiality maintained when requested
- Response within 48 hours for urgent matters
- Follow-up on all concerns raised
- Documentation of conversations (when appropriate)

Communication Channels:
- Regular team meetings and all-hands
- Quarterly town halls with leadership
- Anonymous feedback channels
- Employee surveys (annual and pulse surveys)
- Suggestion box (physical and digital)
- Direct access to HR for concerns

PERFORMANCE MANAGEMENT

Performance Management Philosophy:
- Continuous feedback and development
- Clear expectations and goals
- Regular check-ins and reviews
- Support for improvement
- Recognition of achievements
- Fair and objective evaluation

Goal Setting:
- SMART goals (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
- Aligned with company and department objectives
- Reviewed quarterly with manager
- Updated as priorities change
- Documented in performance management system
- Cascading goals from company to individual

Performance Review Cycle:
- Annual formal review in December
- Mid-year check-in in June
- Quarterly goal reviews
- Continuous feedback throughout year
- Self-assessment component
- 360-degree feedback (optional)
- Development planning included

Performance Ratings:
- Exceeds Expectations: Exceptional performance, exceeds all goals
- Meets Expectations: Solid performance, meets all goals
- Needs Improvement: Below expectations, improvement plan required
- Unsatisfactory: Significant performance issues, formal action required

PERFORMANCE IMPROVEMENT PLANS

Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) Process:
- Initiated when performance is below expectations
- Clear expectations and measurable goals
- Regular check-ins and progress reviews
- Support and resources provided
- Timeline: Typically 30-90 days
- Documentation of progress and outcomes

PIP Components:
- Specific performance issues identified
- Clear expectations and success criteria
- Action plan with milestones
- Support resources and training
- Regular review meetings scheduled
- Consequences if improvement not achieved

PIP Outcomes:
- Successful improvement: Return to normal performance
- Partial improvement: Extended timeline or modified plan
- No improvement: Disciplinary action up to termination
- Employee can appeal PIP decision

DISCIPLINARY PROCEDURES

Disciplinary Action Levels:
1. Verbal Warning: Informal discussion, not documented
2. Written Warning: Formal documentation, placed in file
3. Final Written Warning: Last chance before termination
4. Suspension: Temporary removal from work (with or without pay)
5. Termination: Employment ended

Progressive Discipline:
- Most violations follow progressive discipline
- Each level builds on previous actions
- Documentation at each step
- Opportunity to improve at each level
- Serious violations may skip levels

Serious Violations (May Result in Immediate Termination):
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats of violence
- Harassment or discrimination
- Substance abuse at work
- Safety violations
- Confidentiality breaches
- Falsification of records

Disciplinary Process:
1. Investigation of alleged violation
2. Meeting with employee to discuss concerns
3. Employee opportunity to respond
4. Decision on disciplinary action
5. Documentation of action taken
6. Follow-up and monitoring

CONFLICT RESOLUTION

Conflict Resolution Process:
- Address conflicts early before escalation
- Open communication between parties
- Mediation available through HR
- Formal grievance process if needed
- Investigation of serious conflicts
- Resolution and follow-up

Mediation Services:
- Neutral third-party mediator (HR or external)
- Voluntary participation by all parties
- Confidential process
- Focus on finding solutions
- Agreement documented
- Follow-up to ensure resolution

Grievance Procedure:
- Informal resolution first (discuss with manager)
- Formal written grievance if informal fails
- HR investigation of grievance
- Response within 10 business days
- Appeal process available
- Final decision by senior management

WORKPLACE INVESTIGATIONS

Investigation Process:
- Prompt investigation of complaints
- Impartial investigator assigned
- Confidentiality maintained
- Interviews with all relevant parties
- Review of documents and evidence
- Written report with findings
- Appropriate action taken based on findings

Investigation Timeline:
- Investigation begins within 48 hours
- Completed within 10-15 business days
- Complex cases may take longer
- Updates provided to complainant
- Confidentiality maintained throughout

Investigation Outcomes:
- Complaint substantiated: Disciplinary action taken
- Complaint unsubstantiated: No action, but concerns addressed
- Inconclusive: Additional monitoring or training
- Retaliation prevention measures implemented

EMPLOYEE COMPLAINTS

Types of Complaints:
- Harassment or discrimination
- Workplace safety concerns
- Policy violations
- Unfair treatment
- Manager behavior
- Co-worker conflicts
- Working conditions

Complaint Process:
1. Report complaint to HR or manager
2. Initial assessment of complaint
3. Investigation if warranted
4. Resolution and action taken
5. Follow-up to ensure resolution
6. Prevention measures implemented

No Retaliation Policy:
- Strict prohibition on retaliation
- Protection for complainants and witnesses
- Disciplinary action for retaliation
- Multiple reporting channels available
- Confidentiality maintained
- Support provided throughout process

EMPLOYEE ASSISTANCE

Employee Assistance Program (EAP):
- Free and confidential counseling
- Available 24/7 via phone or online
- 6 sessions per issue per year
- Covers: stress, anxiety, depression, relationship issues
- Legal and financial counseling
- Child and elder care resources

Work-Life Balance Support:
- Flexible work arrangements
- Time off for personal matters
- Support during difficult times
- Referrals to community resources
- Accommodations for disabilities
- Support for caregivers

ACCOMMODATIONS

Reasonable Accommodations:
- Provided for disabilities and medical conditions
- Interactive process with employee
- Medical documentation may be required
- Accommodations evaluated on case-by-case basis
- Undue hardship exception applies
- Confidentiality maintained

Accommodation Process:
1. Employee requests accommodation
2. Documentation reviewed (if needed)
3. Interactive discussion of needs
4. Accommodation options explored
5. Accommodation implemented
6. Regular review and adjustment

Types of Accommodations:
- Modified work schedule
- Physical workspace modifications
- Assistive technology
- Job restructuring
- Leave of absence
- Modified duties
- Policy exceptions

LEAVE OF ABSENCE

Types of Leave:
- Medical leave (FMLA, state, company)
- Family leave (FMLA, state, company)
- Personal leave (unpaid)
- Military leave (USERRA)
- Bereavement leave
- Jury duty leave

Leave Request Process:
1. Submit request to HR with documentation
2. HR reviews eligibility
3. Approval or denial communicated
4. Leave begins on approved date
5. Return-to-work process
6. Job protection during leave

FMLA Eligibility:
- 12 months of service
- 1,250 hours worked in past 12 months
- Up to 12 weeks unpaid leave
- Job protection guaranteed
- Health benefits continue
- Intermittent leave available

RETURN TO WORK

Return-to-Work Process:
- Medical clearance may be required
- Accommodations assessed if needed
- Gradual return schedule available
- Support during transition
- Regular check-ins
- Full duties resumed when ready

TERMINATION

Voluntary Termination:
- Two weeks notice requested
- Exit interview conducted
- Final paycheck processed
- Benefits continuation options
- Return of company property
- Access revoked

Involuntary Termination:
- Clear reason for termination
- Final paycheck and benefits information
- Return of company property
- Exit interview (if appropriate)
- Non-disparagement agreements (if applicable)
- Reference policy explained

Termination Process:
1. Decision made by management
2. HR consultation and review
3. Meeting scheduled with employee
4. Termination communicated clearly
5. Final paperwork completed
6. Access revoked and property returned
