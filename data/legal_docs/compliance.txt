COMPLIANCE AND REGULATIONS

GDPR COMPLIANCE (EU General Data Protection Regulation)

Data Protection Principles:
- We comply with EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)
- Data processing agreements available for all customers
- Lawful basis required for all data processing
- Data minimization: Only collect necessary data
- Purpose limitation: Data used only for stated purposes
- Storage limitation: Data retained only as long as necessary
- Accuracy: Data kept accurate and up to date
- Security: Appropriate technical and organizational measures
- Accountability: Documentation of compliance measures

Individual Rights Under GDPR:
- Right to access: Request copy of personal data
- Right to rectification: Correct inaccurate data
- Right to erasure: Right to be forgotten
- Right to restrict processing: Limit how data is used
- Right to data portability: Receive data in machine-readable format
- Right to object: Object to certain types of processing
- Right to withdraw consent: Withdraw consent at any time
- Right to lodge complaint: File complaint with supervisory authority

Data Processing:
- Lawful basis documented for all processing activities
- Consent obtained when required (clear and unambiguous)
- Legitimate interests assessed and documented
- Contractual necessity identified and documented
- Legal obligations complied with
- Data processing records maintained
- Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) conducted when required

Data Breach Notification:
- Notify supervisory authority within 72 hours of discovery
- Notify affected individuals without undue delay (if high risk)
- Detailed incident reports maintained
- Remediation procedures in place
- Post-incident reviews conducted
- Lessons learned documented and implemented

Data Transfers:
- Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) used for international transfers
- Adequacy decisions recognized
- Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs) where applicable
- Transfer Impact Assessments conducted
- Safeguards implemented for all transfers

CCPA COMPLIANCE (California Consumer Privacy Act)

California Consumer Privacy Act:
- We comply with CCPA requirements
- California residents have specific rights
- Privacy notices updated to include CCPA disclosures
- Opt-out mechanisms provided
- Non-discrimination policy in place

CCPA Consumer Rights:
- Right to know: What personal information is collected, used, shared
- Right to delete: Request deletion of personal information
- Right to opt-out: Opt-out of sale of personal information (we don't sell data)
- Right to non-discrimination: Not discriminated against for exercising rights
- Right to correct: Correct inaccurate personal information
- Right to limit use: Limit use of sensitive personal information

CCPA Disclosures:
- Categories of personal information collected
- Sources of personal information
- Business purposes for collection
- Third parties with whom information is shared
- Methods for exercising rights

CCPA Implementation:
- Privacy policy updated with CCPA disclosures
- Consumer request procedures in place
- Verification processes for requests
- Response timelines: 45 days (can extend 45 days with notice)
- No fee for requests (unless excessive)

SOC 2 COMPLIANCE

Security Controls:
- Annual SOC 2 Type II audits conducted
- Access controls and authentication implemented
- Encryption in transit and at rest
- Regular security monitoring and logging
- Incident response procedures documented and tested
- Change management processes in place
- Vendor management program established

SOC 2 Trust Service Criteria:
- Security: Systems protected against unauthorized access
- Availability: Systems available for operation and use
- Processing Integrity: Processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely
- Confidentiality: Confidential information is protected
- Privacy: Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed in accordance with commitments

Audit Reports:
- SOC 2 reports available under NDA
- Annual audit completion and distribution
- Continuous monitoring between audits
- Third-party security assessments conducted
- Remediation of findings tracked and completed

SOC 2 Benefits:
- Demonstrates security commitment to customers
- Provides assurance of controls effectiveness
- Required by many enterprise customers
- Competitive advantage in marketplace
- Foundation for other compliance requirements

DATA RETENTION AND DELETION

Retention Policies:
- Customer data: Retained while account active
- Inactive accounts: Deleted after 90 days of inactivity
- Legal holds: Extended retention when required by law
- Backup retention: 90 days for automated backups
- Log retention: 1 year for security and audit logs
- Financial records: 7 years (legal requirement)
- Employee records: 7 years after termination
- Marketing data: Until opt-out or account deletion

Data Deletion:
- Deletion requests processed within 30 days
- Complete removal from all systems
- Backup deletion may take additional time (up to 90 days)
- Confirmation provided upon completion
- Cannot delete data required by law (legal holds)
- Anonymization option available for some data

Data Minimization:
- Only collect data necessary for business purposes
- Regular reviews of data collection practices
- Unnecessary data deleted promptly
- Data retention schedules reviewed annually
- Compliance with retention requirements verified

SECURITY INCIDENTS AND BREACH RESPONSE

Incident Response:
- 24/7 security monitoring in place
- Incident response team established
- Containment procedures documented
- Investigation and remediation processes
- Post-incident review and lessons learned
- Communication plans for incidents

Incident Classification:
- Critical: Immediate response required, significant impact
- High: Response within 1 hour, moderate impact
- Medium: Response within 4 hours, limited impact
- Low: Response within 24 hours, minimal impact

Breach Notification:
- Regulatory notifications: Within required timeframes (72 hours for GDPR)
- Customer notifications: Without undue delay if high risk
- Law enforcement: When required by law
- Public disclosure: When required or appropriate
- Notification content: Details of breach, potential impact, remediation steps

Incident Response Process:
1. Detection and identification
2. Containment (immediate and long-term)
3. Eradication of threat
4. Recovery and restoration
5. Post-incident activities
6. Lessons learned and improvements

Reporting:
- Report security issues to security@company.com
- Bug bounty program available for researchers
- Responsible disclosure policy in place
- Recognition and rewards for valid reports
- Coordinated vulnerability disclosure process
